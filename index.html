Your Personal Strength Regimen

A low-impact plan to build strength and support your spine safely.

Important: A Note on Your Safety

This exercise plan is for informational purposes only. Given your specific spinal conditions, it is **essential that you consult with your doctor or a physical therapist** before beginning this or any new exercise program. They can provide guidance tailored to your unique circumstances.

The Golden Rules for Your Workouts:

  • NEVER Push Through Sharp Pain: Listen to your body. If you feel any sharp, shooting, or radiating pain, stop the exercise immediately.
  • Focus on Form, Not Speed: Slow, controlled movements are far more effective and safer than fast, jerky ones.
  • Engage Your Core: Before each movement, gently tighten your abdominal muscles as if you're bracing for a light punch. This creates a "corset" of support for your spine.
  • Breathe: Don't hold your breath. Exhale on the most strenuous part of the exercise.
  • Consistency is Key: Aim for gentle, consistent activity rather than infrequent, intense workouts.

Cardio (Choose One)

Treadmill

20-30 minutes of walking. Keep the incline at 0% to start. Focus on a steady pace.

Elliptical

20-30 minutes at a comfortable resistance. The elliptical is excellent as it has minimal impact.

Core Finisher (Choose Two)

Select two core exercises from the Strength A or B lists (e.g., Pelvic Tilts, Bird-Dog, or Dead Bug) and perform 2 sets of each.

Pilates Board Options

Footwork

Lie on your back and place your feet on the board. Gently press out and slowly return. This strengthens legs with no spinal loading.

Arm Work

Use the straps for bicep curls and rows while lying down, which keeps your spine completely supported.
